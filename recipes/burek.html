Burek

Meat Burek: A Bosnian delicacy.

Burek is a savory pastry popular across the Balkans and the Middle East, made with thin layers of dough filled with ingredients like minced meat, cheese, or vegetables. It's known for its flaky texture and rich, satisfying taste. Burek can be shaped into spirals, coils, or layered pies and is typically baked until golden brown and crispy on the outside.

Making a burek pastry begins with preparing the dough, though some modern recipes opt for store-bought phyllo. Traditional burek dough is made by mixing flour, water, salt, and a touch of oil into a soft, elastic dough. After kneading, the dough is rested, then divided into smaller balls and brushed with oil to prevent drying. Each ball is then stretched carefully and skillfully by hand into thin, nearly transparent sheets. Then it is baked and served for everyone's enjoyment.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or lamb (or a mix)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp paprika (optional)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin (optional)
  • ¼ cup oil or melted butter (for brushing layers)
  • 1 pack phyllo dough (or homemade if preferred)
  • Water (optional, for moistening)

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sauté the onion in a bit of oil until soft. Add the ground meat and spices. Cook until browned. Let cool slightly.
  3. Prepare your baking tray by oiling it lightly.
  4. Layer the phyllo sheets: Place one sheet, brush with oil or butter, and repeat 2-3 times.
  5. Add filling: Spoon some meat mixture along one edge and roll, or layer it between sheets depending on your style (spiral, layered, or cigar shape).
  6. Arrange in tray and repeat until you use up the filling.
  7. Optional: Lightly sprinkle water over the top to keep it moist.
  8. Bake for 30-40 minutes or until golden and crispy.
  9. Cool slightly, cut into pieces, and enjoy with plain yogurt or ayran!
