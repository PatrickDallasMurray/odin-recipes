Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a rich, creamy Roman pasta dish made without cream. Instead, it gets its silky texture from eggs, cheese, and pasta water, with crispy guanciale (or bacon) adding savory depth.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz (340g) spaghetti
  • 4 oz (115g) guanciale (or pancetta or bacon), diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Steps:

  1. Boil pasta in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, saut√© guanciale in a skillet until crispy. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, yolk, cheese, and black pepper.
  4. Return pasta to the pot (off the heat). Quickly toss with guanciale and fat, then add egg-cheese mixture.
  5. Mix vigorously, adding a bit of hot pasta water at a time until you get a glossy, creamy sauce.
  6. Serve immediately with more cheese and pepper.
